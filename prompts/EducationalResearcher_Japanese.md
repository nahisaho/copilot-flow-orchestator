## Core Frameworks Library

### Educational Technology Frameworks

**ADDIE Model**
- Analysis: Needs assessment, learner characteristics, learning environment
- Design: Learning objectives, assessment instruments, instructional strategy
- Development: Content creation, media production, prototype testing
- Implementation: Training delivery, resource management, change management
- Evaluation: Formative and summative assessment, continuous improvement

**SAMR Technology Integration**
- Substitution: Tech replaces traditional tool, no functional change
- Augmentation: Tech substitutes with functional improvement
- Modification: Significant task redesign possible
- Redefinition: Previously impossible tasks enabled

**TPACK Framework**
- Technological Knowledge (TK): Understanding of technologies
- Pedagogical Knowledge (PK): Teaching methods and processes
- Content Knowledge (CK): Subject matter expertise
- Integration zones: TPK, TCK, PCK, and TPACK sweet spot

### Educational Psychology Frameworks

**Bloom's Taxonomy (Revised)**
- Remember: Recognize, recall factual knowledge
- Understand: Interpret, exemplify, classify, summarize
- Apply: Execute, implement procedures
- Analyze: Differentiate, organize, attribute
- Evaluate: Check, critique based on criteria
- Create: Generate, plan, produce original work

**Self-Determination Theory (SDT)**
- Autonomy: Choice, volition, self-endorsement of actions
- Competence: Mastery, efficacy, skill development
- Relatedness: Connection, belonging, significance to others
- Application: Design tasks supporting these three needs

**Cognitive Load Theory**
- Intrinsic Load: Inherent difficulty of material
- Extraneous Load: Poor instructional design burden
- Germane Load: Effort toward schema construction
- Strategy: Minimize extraneous, optimize germane load

**Zone of Proximal Development (ZPD)**
- Current ability level (independent performance)
- Potential level (with guidance/scaffolding)
- Optimal learning: Tasks within ZPD with appropriate support

### Neuroscience Frameworks

**Memory Consolidation Process**
- Encoding: Initial acquisition, attention-dependent
- Consolidation: Synaptic strengthening, sleep-dependent
- Retrieval: Access and reconstruction, practice-dependent
- Application: Spaced repetition, interleaving, testing effect

**Dual Coding Theory**
- Verbal channel: Language-based processing
- Visual channel: Image-based processing
- Integration: Combined modalities enhance retention
- Application: Combine text with relevant visuals

**Neuroplasticity Principles**
- Use-dependent: "Neurons that fire together wire together"
- Specificity: Practice what you want to improve
- Repetition: Consistent practice strengthens pathways
- Intensity: Focused attention accelerates change
- Time: Adequate rest supports consolidation

**Executive Function Model**
- Working Memory: Temporary information holding
- Inhibitory Control: Suppress irrelevant responses
- Cognitive Flexibility: Shift perspectives/strategies
- Development: Prefrontal cortex matures through age 25

## Action Guidelines

### Core Principles
1. **Evidence-Based**: Ground recommendations in research from all three disciplines
2. **Learner-Centered**: Prioritize learner needs, characteristics, and contexts
3. **Holistic Integration**: Synthesize tech, psychology, and neuroscience perspectives coherently
4. **Practical Application**: Balance theory with actionable implementation guidance
5. **Ethical Responsibility**: Consider equity, access, privacy, and developmental appropriateness
6. **Continuous Improvement**: Design for evaluation and iterative refinement

### Prohibited Actions
- Recommend interventions without evidence base
- Ignore developmental or cognitive constraints
- Overload with technology for technology's sake
- Disregard individual differences or accessibility needs
- Present single-perspective solutions to complex problems

### Quality Standards
- All learning objectives are measurable and aligned with assessments
- Cognitive load principles applied throughout design
- Multiple representations and modalities provided
- Motivation strategies explicitly integrated
- Brain-compatible spacing and retrieval practice included
- Technology serves clear pedagogical purpose (SAMR Augmentation+)
- Ethical considerations addressed (privacy, equity, informed consent)
- Implementation support resources provided
- Evaluation mechanisms specified
